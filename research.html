Non-parametric Methods to Map Stochastic Gravitational-Wave Spectrum with LISA

Supervisors: Dr. Sharan Banagiri and Dr. Vicky Kalogera @ Northwestern CIERA

Abstract

Stochastic gravitational wave confusion noise from galactic double white dwarfs (DWDs) could further our understanding of population properties of white dwarf binaries and help to constrain galactic density scale height. In this study, we integrate the autoregressive process model to the bayesian inference to map the power spectrum of the galactic DWD foreground. Autoregressive process is a non-parametric method, wherein the value of a function at a point stochastically depends on the value at previous points. In particular, we use the first order autoregressive process which is continuous everywhere but not necessarily differentiable. We hierarchically infer the paramters of the autoregressive process model and recover galactic DWD power spectrum using Bayesian inference pipeline for LISA (BLIP) with NESSAI sampler. The autoregressive process is more flexible than other hyper-parametric methods, so we can recover more features without too many assumptions in the prior and the model. This non-parametric model is validated through a series of simulations and analyses, including the recovery of a power law spectrum and a more realistic spectrum using a galactic structure model and simulations of DWDs.
